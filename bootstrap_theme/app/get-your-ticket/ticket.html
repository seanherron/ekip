About the Every Kid in a Park Annual Pass

1. The Every Kid in a Park Annual Pass is FREE.

2. The pass is only available to students in the 4th grade during the 2015-2016 school year.

3. The pass may be obtained by exchanging the voucher in person at Federal recreation sites that charge entrance or standard amenity fees.

4. The pass covers entrance to Fish and Wildlife Service and National Park Service sites that charge Entrance Fees, and Standard Amenity Fees at Forest Service, Bureau of Land Management and Bureau of Reclamation sites.

5. The pass admits the 4th grader (the pass owner) and any accompanying passengers in a private non-commercial vehicle at per vehicle fee areas, or the pass owner and up to additional 3 adults at sites that charge per person.

6. The pass is non-transferable.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What are the benefits for the Every Kid in a Park Pass for 4th Graders?

Free admission at sites that charge entrance or standard amenity fees for September 2015-August 2016.

o Allows pass owner and accompanying passengers in a single, private, non-commercial vehicle to enter federally operated recreation sites across the country.

o Covers the pass owner and three (3) accompanying adults age 16 and older at sites where per person entrance fees are charged. No entry fee charged for children 15 and under.

The pass does not cover or provide a discount on expanded amenity fees such as camping, boat launch or interpretive fees.

2. Who qualifies for the Every Kid in a Park Pass for 4th Graders?

Only 4th graders during the 2015-2016 are eligible for the pass.

3. Where can I use my pass?

Please contact a site directly if you have a question about pass acceptance and fees. The Forest Service, the National Park Service, Fish and Wildlife Service, Bureau of Land Management, and Bureau of Reclamation honor the pass at sites where Entrance or Standard Amenity Fee(s) are charged.

o Bureau of Reclamation http://www.usbr.gov

o Fish and Wildlife Service http://www.fws.gov

US Army Corps of Engineers will not be issuing this pass in the inaugural year, however they may honor it for free access at some sites. For more information contact your local US Army Corps of Engineers.
